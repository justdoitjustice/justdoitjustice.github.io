About

Artificial intelligence and robots appeared in movies long before they came into existence. In fact, “Gugusse et l'automaton / Gugusse and the Automaton”, made in 1897, is the first movie to depict robots (Zed Fest). Throughout the years, insubordination and world demolition appear to be underlying themes in movies depicting robots. Although much of it is dramatized, there appears to be overlaying examples where cinematic representation align with reality. AI has since been depicted in hundreds of different ways throughout the history of cinema. These iterations vary every time, showing the many different ways AI can either benefit man greatly, or reek utter havoc. Whether it be the friendly robot in The Iron Giant, or the evil computer HAL-9000 in 2001: A Space Odyssey, directors and writers clearly have different opinions on how these machines can fit into our world. Here are some of the different ways AI has been shown all over mainstream media

Types of Robots in Movies

Robots Gone Bad

A classic way of depicting AI and robots in movies to simply depict them as bad guys whose purpose to hinder man which is the exact opposite reason they were created for in the first place. One of the greatest examples of this was HAL-9000 from Stanley Kubrik's 2001: A Space Odyssey in which a computer goes on a murderous rampage after realizing that humans were going to shut it down because it was malfunctioning. Above is one of the most famous scenes in the movie when HAL first turns against the people aboard a spacecraft.

Transportation Robots

When you think of automatic transportation in the film industry the transformers usually come to mind. They are cars who drive themselves around and fight crime. Although the vigilante aspect of transformers had yet to be seen in the real world, fully automated vehicles do exist in today’s society, and have made transportation much easier. Vehicle companies such as Tesla have been using self-driving technology to allow their customers to get from destination to destination without even having to move the steering wheel. They learn as they drive so although not perfect they will never make the same mistake twice. One day in the future automated cars might be so reliable that passengers could fall asleep in the wheel and feel safe.

Informative Robots

Star Trek, taking place in year 2364 and beyond, serves as an emblem for artificial intelligence as much of the advanced technologies depicted in the show came true in reality. One example is seen through communicators. “Kirk and Spock would use natural language to request that the Enterprise computer perform complex data-mining tasks nearly instantaneously,” (Techrepublic). This is prevalent today through computer assistants such as Siri, Cortana, and Alexa. Another example of technology in the show that came to life is seen through the visor in which Geordi La Forge wears to enable him to see. Today, we have JORDY, which stands for Joint Optical Reflective Display. The headset magnifies objects 50 times and users can adjust the contrast and brightness. It is meant for people with low vision= defined by Pcworld as “sight that can’t be fixed by conventional glasses”. An overlying theme in fictitious television shows seems to be the portrayal of sentient technology (humanism) that turns wrong. “Captain Kirk's job is replaced by a machine driven by artificial intelligence—and the AI turns out to be out of control and murderous,” (Techrepublic).

Emotional Robots

In a pessimistic future, society has abandoned earth due to the insurmountable pollution caused by humans. Wall-E is a robot left behind on earth left to clean up the mess in order to hopefully make earth habitable again one day. Wall-E shows a great array of emotions for an AI machine he likes to listen to music watch romantic films and even has a pet cockroach. When he meets Eve he develops the ability to love and even though all he can say is his name he expresses his love very similar to humans. He holds eves hand, comforts her, and eventually even kisses her. When Eve gets shut off you can see Wall-E feels sad as he protects her from the rain in her comatose state. In today’s society a robot named Pepper has been made to understand our speech and emotion due to the tone of the voice. This robot is meant to be a home assistant that can understand how you feel and talk to their owner. It is just a prototype at the moment but it seems emotional robots are in our near future.

Conclusion

There are pros and cons to the development of artificial intelligence. Overall, we believe that it will offer a net positive. Artificial intelligence will augment our way of life (i.e. through smart homes that offer not only more safety and security but also an eco-friendly solution through energy reduction). On the other hand, some advanced technologies that are seemingly beneficial can also offer the prospect of a daunting future. One of these technologies includes CRISPR. A “CRISPR” (pronounced “crisper”) stand for Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats, which are the hallmark of a bacterial defense system that forms the basis for CRISPR-Cas9 genome editing technology,” (Feng Zheng). It is the most revolutionary biological discovery of this century as scientists have discovered a novel mechanism bacteria use to thwart viral infections. It saves DNA signature and enables people to snip out DNA, kill it, replace it (edit) with something else, etc (Megan Molteni). Therefore, you can now have an embryo, minus a disease, that has afflicted the family for generations. Not only is it extremely efficient and effective, but it is easy enough to create DIY kit novices. However, what about nefarious intentions? Novice may mess around with a virus or bacteria and create a superbug that kills billions, or lead to extinction or plants or insects can be created with hybrid DNA that runs rampant, destroy ecosystems, and causes mass famine. The possibilities are endless and can be done with exceptional ease.
